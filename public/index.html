How QR Codes Work

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can store URLs, text, and other data. When scanned with a smartphone camera, they instantly open the encoded information. QR codes were invented in 1994 by Denso Wave and have become the standard for connecting physical and digital experiences. They're used everywhere — from product packaging and restaurant menus to business cards and event tickets.
